Thinking Fast and Slow

About : Thinking, Fast and Slow by Daniel Kahneman delves into the two modes of thought that drive human decision-making: System 1 (fast, intuitive thinking) and System 2 (slow, deliberate thinking). Kahneman, a Nobel Prize-winning psychologist, explains how these systems influence our judgments, biases, and choices. The book explores cognitive biases, heuristics, and common errors in thinking that affect everything from everyday decisions to major life choices. By understanding how the mind operates, readers can make more rational decisions, avoid cognitive traps, and better control their behavior. Thinking, Fast and Slow is widely regarded as a landmark work in behavioral economics and psychology.
