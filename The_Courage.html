The Courage to be Disliked

About : The Courage to Be Disliked by Ichiro Kishimi and Fumitake Koga is a philosophical dialogue based on the teachings of Alfred Adler, one of the founding figures of modern psychology. The book presents a conversation between a philosopher and a young man, exploring Adlerian principles such as the idea that happiness comes from accepting oneself and breaking free from the need for approval from others. The core message revolves around individual freedom, self-responsibility, and the courage to live authentically. It challenges conventional beliefs about trauma, relationships, and social pressure, urging readers to embrace discomfort and take control of their own happiness.
